The effective and fair distribution of healthcare resources continues to be a significant challenge for health systems globally, particularly within England's National Health Service (NHS). To address these challenges, this thesis presents a mixed-methods framework that combines Data Envelopment Analysis (DEA) for quantitative assessment of efficiency with behavioural principles to reflect human and organizational dynamics in the allocation of resources. This research evaluates hospitals based on financial and operational metrics; it also assesses the influence of cognitive biases and organizational culture on analytical results. The study demonstrates that integrating DEA with behavioural intelligence results in models that are analytically sound, contextually relevant, and practical. By connecting technical evaluation and practical decision-making, the results offer direction for policy and management, promoting transparent, flexible, and equitable distribution of NHS resources.
